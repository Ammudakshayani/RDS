Introduction

AP YSR Pedalandariki Illu Scheme is a housing scheme launched by the Andhra Pradesh government under the leadership of Chief Minister Y.S. Jagan Mohan Reddy. The scheme aims to provide pucca houses to the homeless and landless poor families living in the rural areas of the state. In this blog, we will discuss the key features and benefits of the scheme.

Key Features of the scheme

Under the AP YSR Pedalandariki Illu Scheme, the state government will provide financial assistance of Rs. 1.5 lakhs to each eligible beneficiary for the construction of a pucca house. The beneficiaries can use this amount to construct a house of their choice with a minimum plinth area of 300 square feet. The houses will have basic amenities such as electricity, water supply, and sanitation facilities.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the scheme, the following criteria must be fulfilled:
• The beneficiary should be a permanent resident of Andhra Pradesh.
• The beneficiary should belong to a landless and homeless family in the rural areas of the state.
• The beneficiary should not own any pucca house in his/her name or in the name of any family member.

Benifits of the Scheme

The AP YSR Pedalandariki Illu Scheme has several benefits for the homeless and landless poor families in the rural areas of Andhra Pradesh. Some of the key benefits are:
• It provides a pucca house to the homeless and landless poor families, giving them a sense of security and dignity.
• It improves the living conditions of the beneficiaries, who otherwise have to live in substandard and unsafe housing conditions.
• It helps in reducing poverty by providing a permanent asset to the beneficiaries.
• It boosts the rural economy by creating employment opportunities in the construction sector.

Challenges Faced

The implementation of the AP YSR Pedalandariki Illu Scheme has not been without its challenges. Some of the key challenges faced by the government in implementing the scheme are:
• Identification of beneficiaries: The identification of eligible beneficiaries is a complex process, and there have been reports of irregularities in the selection process in some areas.
• Availability of land: In some areas, the beneficiaries do not have access to land on which they can construct the house.
• Quality of construction: There have been concerns about the quality of construction of the houses and whether they are built as per the standards set by the government.

Implementation of the Scheme

The AP YSR Pedalandariki Illu Scheme is being implemented in a phased manner across all the districts of Andhra Pradesh. The beneficiaries are identified through a survey conducted by the Gram Panchayats and the Rural Development Department. The beneficiaries are selected based on the eligibility criteria and their economic and social status.
Progress of the Scheme Since the launch of the scheme in 2019, the Andhra Pradesh government has made significant progress in implementing the scheme. As of April 2023, more than 10 lakh beneficiaries have been identified, and around 6.5 lakh houses have been sanctioned. Out of these sanctioned houses, around 3.5 lakh houses have been completed and handed over to the beneficiaries. The remaining houses are under various stages of construction.




Steps Taken to Address The Challenges

The Andhra Pradesh government has taken several steps to address the challenges faced in implementing the scheme. Some of the key steps taken are:
• Use of technology: The government has used the latest technology, such as drones and GIS mapping, to identify the beneficiaries and their landholdings.
• Provision of land: In areas where the beneficiaries do not have access to land, the government is providing land through the Land Pooling Scheme and other initiatives.
• Quality checks: The government has set up a system of quality checks to ensure that the houses are built as per the standards set by the government.

Conclusion

The AP YSR Pedalandariki Illu Scheme is a crucial initiative by the Andhra Pradesh government to provide affordable housing to the homeless and landless poor families in the rural areas of the state. The scheme has the potential to improve the living conditions of the beneficiaries and boost the rural economy. While there have been some challenges in implementing the scheme, the government’s efforts to address them are commendable. The scheme has the potential to serve as a model for other states in the country to follow.
